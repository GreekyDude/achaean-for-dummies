The Syllabary

59 core syllabic signs, verified against the Unicode Linear B block (U+10000–U+1003F) and kinezika.com

Linear B is a syllabary, not an alphabet. Each sign represents a syllable of the form V (vowel alone) or CV (consonant + vowel). It cannot directly represent consonant clusters or word-final consonants — scribal rules handle these limitations. Click any sign to see its details, notes, and an attested example from the tablets.

The Grid System

Signs are organised by vowel column (a, e, i, o, u) and consonant row. Some cells are empty — those CV combinations either didn't exist or weren't needed in Mycenaean. The q-series (labio-velars) and w-series (digamma) disappeared entirely from later Greek.
