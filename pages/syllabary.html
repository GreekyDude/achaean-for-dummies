The Syllabary

59 core signs · verified against Unicode Linear B block (U+10000–U+1003F) and kinezika.com

Linear B is a syllabary, not an alphabet. Each sign represents a syllable of the form V (vowel) or CV (consonant + vowel). Click any sign to see its phonetic value, notes, and an attested example from the tablets.

The Grid System

Signs are organised by vowel column (a, e, i, o, u) and consonant row. Some cells are empty — those CV combinations either didn't exist or weren't needed. The q-series (labio-velars kʷ/gʷ) and w-series (digamma Ϝ) both disappeared from later Greek.
