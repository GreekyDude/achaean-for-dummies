The Number System

Linear B uses a decimal system. Signs are additive — stack them to build any number.

Numbers in Linear B are written with Aegean numerals (Unicode block U+10100+). The system is strictly additive and decimal: 𐄇 = 1, 𐄙 = 10, 𐄢 = 100, 𐄫 = 1000. To write 34, you write 𐄙𐄙𐄙𐄊 (three 10s + one 4-sign). Numbers always follow the commodity ideogram they quantify.

How numbers appear on tablets

A typical tablet entry reads: [recipient] [commodity ideogram] [number]. For example, the line 𐀷𐀙𐀏 𐄫𐄢𐄙𐄇 would mean "wanax (king): 1,111 [units of grain]". The ideogram tells you what is being counted even if you can't read the words around it.
